Sampling

Here we demonstrate the effect of our sampling algorithm in two 2D scenes and one 3D scene. The figures below showcase our sampled boundary paths of length 1 and 2, but our algorithm also works for any length.

2D Spheres

For the figure on the left, the dot at the upper left denotes the camera, the segment at the upper right denotes an area light, and each yellow cross denotes the intersection point of an boundary path that we sampled during forward rendering. The grey deshed lines help make clear the boundary conditions. For the figure on the right, we plot directly all the boundary paths of length 1 and 2 that we sampled, with each boundary paths having a different color.

3D Bunny

The image on the right shows boundary paths of length 1 for the bunny. The intersection points of the boundary paths are colored and then projected back to the image plane. Red denotes that the camera ray is tangential to the bunny; green denotes boundary paths due to self occulusion; and blue denotes that the light ray is tangential to the bunny.
